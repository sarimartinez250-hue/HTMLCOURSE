Love quotes in other languages

Love is one of, if not the greatest, feelings that can be experienced on this earth, which is why we have compiled a list of phrases from other languages ​​around the world so you can express your love in every language.

For example, the phrase: Amor, sicut umbra, non habet finem. It comes from Latin and means: Love, like a shadow, has not end.

Furthermore, we can cite more phrases like the one above, which, translated from their native language, have deep meanings and emotions.

French

  • L'amour n'est pas bon ni mauvais, mais est mieux avec toi. From frances and it means: Love is neither good nor bad, but it's better with you.

Italian

  • Per il mondo tu sei soltanto cualcuna, per me tu sei il mondo. It comes from Italian and means: To the world you are more, to me, you are the world

Russian

  • ЖелаЎ всего хорошего This another one comes from Russian, this is not a easy language, but your partner will surely appreciate the effort you put in. And this phrase means: I wish you the greatest happiness.

German

  • Germans are known for being cold and rigid people, but they also have a soft heart and beautiful expressions like: Du hast mir ein Lächeln auf das Gesicht gezauber which means: You draw a smile on my face.

Latin

  • Another one from Latin, if your partner are a philosophy lover: Amor est vitae essentia which means: Love is the essence of life. And it is absolutely right!

Portuguese

  • Portuguese is a very sweet language to the ear , so we couldn't leave it out of our collection of love phrases. E a coisa mais divina que há no mundo; É viver cada segundo como nunca mais which translates to: Love is the most divine thing in the world; it is living every second like never before. This phrase is from the Brazilian composer Vinicius de Moraes, who perfectly described the concept of love.

Arabic

  • Although Arabic can be complicated, it is the fifth most spoken language in the world . This is how it is said 'I want to be with you always' in Arabic: ريد ان اكون معك دائما which means: I want to always be with you

Chinese

  • Do you dare to say "I love you" in Chinese ? In the Chinese transcription system, to make it easier to pronounce, it's written 我爱你。 Easy no? Come on and practice your pronunciation!

Hungarian

  • And the last one from the Hungarian Örökké szeretni foglak which means: I will love you forever.

I really hope you had enjoyed these phrases and try to pronounce every to surprise your partner, I promise you that he/she really appreciate your effort to express your love for her/his in foreign languages!
